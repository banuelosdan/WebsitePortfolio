Assignment 1: Memorandum

Assignment 1 was the first assignment of CPSC 311 at CSUF. It was an individual assignment. For this assignment I had to write to my boss of a fictitious company and persuade them why I deserve a promotion. I argue why I would be better suited to the company as a technical writer. In the memorandum, I provide details about my skills and the importance of technical writing in the work environment. I give personal skills aside from work that will be benefical and skills that have culminated in the work force. My skills are more fit for the technical writing position for the company.

Technical Writing is the importance of this course and for our first assignment we were assigned to research the benefits and properties of techincal writing. I learned how to properly use tags such as headers and how to modify the text without using direct formatting.

This was the first assignment, so naturally, there was a lot of forgiveness. Although, I did learn from my mistakes and prioritized formatting for my future papers and presentations.

Assignment 2: New Student Guide

Assignment 2 was the first group assignment. My group consisted of 3 members: Steven, Kevin, and myself. We were assigned to create a student handguide for new students. We were advised to give special insight information that only current students or alumn would know about the campus to aid the new students. We divided the work into three sections: Quality of Life, Resources, and Academic Aid.

For this project we had to utilize several techniques we had learned in the course. We had to make sure to utilize formatting techniquies as well as writing techniques. We used several methods of structuring our information like outlines and bullet points. The goal of this assignment was to use deductive writing to quickly give out information.

This assignment was our first group assignment, so the grading was still a bit lenient. One thing I learned from this was to use properly create an outline and use hyperlinks to direct the user to certain areas of the paper.

Assignment 3: Group Research Project

Assignment 3 was the second group assignment. My group consisted of 3 members: Steven, Kevin, and myself. This assignment was a research project. We chose to research Arduino boards. We researched what the Arduino Boards are, how they are used, then we compared the boards to other MCU's on the market.

For this project we had to utilize several techniques we had learned in the course. We had to make sure to utilize formatting techniquies as well as writing techniques. Some of the writing technique we had to use was ABC format. ABC format is a writing layout that states what will be said, then we prove our statements, then we state it again. We also used deductive reasoning in our writing style.

This assignment was graded a bit more seriously. One thing I learned from this assignment was how to use tags and how to format paragraphs. Prior to this assignment, I was still directly formatting my work, thinking I was using indirect formatting. I also took away some useful writing advice from this assignment. Such as structuring topics and using subheaders.

Assignment 4: Group Ethics Assignment

Assignment 4 was the third group assignment. My group consisted of 3 members: Steven, Kevin, and myself. This assignment revolved around an Ethics lecture. We were given an ethical dilemma and had to use four ethical theories. We backed up our reasoning with Kantian, Egoism, Virtue, and Utilitarian ethics.

For this project we had to have a clear understanding of each of the four theories and apply them to the dilemma. This assignment was lenient on formatting. We had to prioritize the content and make sure our reasoning was true to the ethical theory.

I learned about various ethical theories and how you can apply them given that you use the correct reasoning. This assignment was graded purely on the content and I learned how important it is to communicate with the group members because if one person does not understand the theory, everyone is marked for the mistake.

Assignment 5: Group Bias Assignment

Assignment 5 was the fourth group assignment. My group consisted of 3 members: Steven, Kevin, and myself. This assignment was another assignment revolving around a bias lecture. The assignment was to find four fallacious arguments each. And apply which fallacy they used.

For my four arguments I used: Red Herring, False Equivalence, Burden of Proof, and Correlation Causation. I used Twitter and Reddit to find my arguments. Finding arguments then analyzing whether they used fallacious reasoning proved to be troublesome.

I learned a lot from this assignment. Again we did not need to prioritize the formatting of the text as much as we normally do. Rather, for this assignment we had to have a strong understanding of numerous fallacies and elaborate on our reasoning to showcase our knowledge on the topic.

Assignment 6: Group Research Presentation

Assignment 6 was the fifth and final group assignment. My group consisted of 3 members: Steven, Kevin, and myself. This assignment was a presentation. We had to present our research paper on Arduino boards. This assignment was informed to us by the professor to be practice for the Final.

For the presentation each of us were given approximately 2 minutes to cover the portion of the paper we researched. I was in charge of the comparison of Arduino boards to other MCU's. No extra work was necessary, all we had to do was convert our paper into a presentation, while still using ABC format and deductive writin.

This presentation was a good warm-up for the Final presentation. We had a chance to work of our performance skills, which include speech, eye-contact, readiness, and overall knowledge of the topics. I made sure to utilize the feedback from this assignment and implement it into my Final. We lacked a concise structure and styling of our presentation.
