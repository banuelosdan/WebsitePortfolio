ABOUT ME

My Educational Journey

I graduated from High School in 2016, without any idea of what I wanted to do in life. My parents never grew up in an educational home, but wanted to change that for myself and my siblings. I am the third oldest child of four. I have two older brothers who attended college, so I felt that I should do the same.

Still unaware of what I wanted to study, I blindly attended my local community college. There I was able to venture into some of my interests. I was always fond of Mathematics and Science, though I never delved into it much more than that. In my time at SBVC I focused primarily on completing general math and science courses, hoping that I would take interest into something less vague. It wasn't until my fourth semester there, I decided to major in Computer Science.

After my fourth semester at SBVC, I applied to CSUF and was admitted for the Fall of 2018. I had only taken one CPSC course at my CC, so I was completely new to the subject. I had no prior education experience, nor had I ever looked into it during my own time. It was an abrupt decision. Eventhough I feel behind in a lot of my classes, I enjoy learning the topics and every semester I seem to be learning even more material than the last. I am happy with my career choice and the destination I chose to pursure it.

Cal State Fullerton has been an amazing school that has taught me a lot about being a better student. I am now in my fourth year as a college student, and my second here at CSUF. I've since taken numerous courses that have made me a better Programmer. I have also met great Professors, who are willing to take time out of their day to teach me new things.

As it currently stands, I am expected to graduate in the Spring of 2021 with a Bachelors in Computer Science. I am close to the finish line, but I know I still have a long way to go, event after graduation, before I can be a great Programmer. I have so much to learn, but I am having the time of my life doing it.

Career Ambitions

One of the main factors that persuaded me into majoring in Computer Science was because of my interest in video games. So, naturally, I studied CS in hopes of one day becoming a Game Developer. Though now that I've been in the CS field for a few years. I understand how unlikely that is. Although, it isn't entirely impossible. But I mention this because, eventhough, game development was the only reason I decided to major in CS, now that I've studied it for a while, I am actually open to a lot of jobs that CS offer. From software development, to networking, to cybersecurity, or web development my range if interest has widened tremendously.

Unfortunately, as it now stands, I do not have my sights locked in on a specific job title, I am hoping that my electives will help me broaden my search. But, I am open to any internships that can come my way to really understand how it feels to work as a programmer.
